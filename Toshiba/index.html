Benefits to Trilayer Supercapacitors

Increased Capacitance

Through the use of the high surface area trilayer, close electrodes, a heterogeneous current collector, and the ABA stacking method the proposed supercapacitor would have a much greater overall capacitance

Thermal Stability

Several components, such as the resin bonded cellulose separator and gel-based ion polymer for the electrolyte, will lead to improvements in thermal stability within the cell

Increased Operational Safety

Unlike batteries, supercapacitors store and release energy as electrostatic charges from the attraction of electrolyte ions to oppositely charged electrodes, forming a charged double layer caled the Helmholtz Layer. The replacement of flammable liquid electrolyte with a polymer-based electrolyte will create a safer device. The charge-discharge cycles will be significantly increased beyond a battery.

Problems with Lithium Ion Batteries

Suboptimal Performance

Lithium-ion batteries are susceptible to low power densities (the rate at which energy is charged/discharged), and less charge/discharge cycles due to electrochemical reactions

Safety Concerns

The electrochemical reactions also pose a threat due to dendrite (lithium tendrils) formation, that if get through the electrolyte and breach the separator, can cause short-circuiting and overheating

World Lithium Shortage

A world shortage has been rumored causing fluctuation in the prices of the alkali metal. Their demand will be increased with the production of electric vehicles that use them in the powertrain.
