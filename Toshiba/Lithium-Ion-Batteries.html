Lithium-Ion Batteries

The basic components of the current design consists of stacks or windings of positive and negative electrodes separated by a thin separator and wet with organic electrolyte containing Lithium ions. One common casing format uses a cylindrical cell and tightly spiraled electrodes, resulting in increased surface area that maximizes capacity.

Downsides to Lithium-Ion Batteries

Lithium ions pre-doped into the negative electrode or Anode move from the negative to positive electrode or Cathode during discharge; the reverse occurs during charging. These reactions are known as redox reactions and are reversible, thus enabling rechargability.
